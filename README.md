# RSE-CEP Content

> **Experimental — Not for production use.** This project and all pattern outputs are prototypes under active development. Content may be incomplete, inaccurate, or change without notice. Do not rely on these patterns for operational decisions.

Content and publishing infrastructure for the [CDL Research Software Engineering Capacity Enhancement Project (RSE-CEP)](https://rse-cep.github.io/rse-cep-content/).

RSE-CEP develops practical guidance for research software engineers working in the humanities, arts, and social sciences (HASS) and Indigenous research contexts — areas where the technical challenges of building research software are inseparable from cultural, ethical, and disciplinary considerations.

## The site

The RSE-CEP website is a structured resource, not just a pattern collection. Patterns are the primary content type, but they sit within a broader architecture: reference architectures, domain guidance, and other organising content that makes the collection navigable and useful in practice.

**Patterns** document solutions to recurring problems in HASS and Indigenous research software engineering, classified into four types:

- **Implementation (I)** — specific technologies, tools, and engineering techniques
- **Architectural (A)** — system structure, data governance, and component relationships
- **Design (D)** — artefact behaviour, interaction design, and specifications
- **Process (P)** — workflows, stakeholder engagement, and human-AI collaboration

See [docs/pattern_typology.md](./docs/pattern_typology.md) for the full typology and classification guide.

## How content is produced

Content is authored through an AI-assisted pipeline: source materials (interview transcripts, talks, documentation) are extracted into structured drafts, reviewed by the team, and published through a PR-based quality gate. See [docs/pattern-pipeline.md](./docs/pattern-pipeline.md) for the full pipeline documentation.
